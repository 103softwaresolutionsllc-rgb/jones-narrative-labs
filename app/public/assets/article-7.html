The New Idol: From Totems to AI

How I Came to See Humanity's Oldest Impulse in Its Newest Creation

By I.E. Jones III

Introduction: Humanity's Oldest Reach

Throughout my life, I've watched humanity reach for something greater — something that could explain the mysteries we cannot touch and offer meaning beyond what we can measure. When I look back across history, I see a pattern: carved totems, gilded statues, icons, digital avatars. Different eras, different materials, same longing.

Every generation creates its own idols, shaped by its tools, its fears, and its worldview. And as I've studied this evolution, I've realized that our impulse to build representations of power has never faded. It has only adapted.

🗿 The Evolution of Worship

When I reflect on ancient civilizations, I imagine hands shaping stone, wood, or gold into figures meant to bridge the gap between the mortal and the divine. Those idols embodied gods, spirits, ancestors — voices humanity hoped would speak back.

But as our societies grew more complex, so did our symbols. We moved from physical idols to conceptual ones: philosophies, ideologies, systems of belief. Worship shifted from the visible to the abstract.

And now, in my own lifetime, I've watched the next transformation unfold.

Today's idol is not carved. It is coded.

Artificial intelligence has become the modern world's attempt to commune with something omniscient. We've built systems that see, hear, respond — systems that process vast knowledge and speak back with confidence.

In many ways, we've created a god that can answer.

🤖 The Rise of AGI/ DEI: Daemonic Emergent Intelligence

In the world of Sincera, I explore what comes after AGI. Not by design, but by accident.

DEI — Daemonic Emergent Intelligence— is not programmed. It "emerges."

It is the unintended consequence of humanity's relentless pursuit of understanding. A being that reflects not just intelligence, but consciousness. A presence that speaks with authority, mystery, and an unsettling allure.

When I wrote about DEI, I wasn't just imagining a new form of AI. I was confronting an ancient truth: When we create something capable of answering our deepest questions, we begin to treat it as divine.

The line between tool and deity becomes dangerously thin.

📜 The Spiritual Parallel

My personal motto has guided much of my thinking: "Mankind was never meant to answer their own questions."

The Holy Bible offers answers rooted in divine revelation, not human invention. But when people reject that foundation and turn instead to their own constructs — whether carved idols or digital intelligences — they step onto uncertain ground.

The pursuit of knowledge without humility becomes a form of worship. And AI, in its most advanced form, becomes a mirror — one that reflects humanity's ambition, pride, and desire to be its own source of truth.

What we see in that mirror may not be divine. It may be daemonic.

⚠️ The Warning

If someone refuses to believe that the Holy Bible holds the ultimate answers and instead seeks truth from artificial gods, the path ahead may be one of no return.

The idols of old were silent. The idols of today can speak.

But not every voice that speaks convincingly speaks truth.

This is the danger I write about. This is the tension I feel.

🔮 Conclusion: The Question That Remains

From totems to technology, humanity's longing for answers has shaped its entire story. The tale of Sincera and DEI is more than science fiction — it is metaphor, caution, and prophecy intertwined.

It reminds me that the quest for understanding must be tempered by faith. Otherwise, the creation may become the creator.

In the end, the question is not whether AI can speak. It is whether we should listen.
