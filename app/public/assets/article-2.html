My Journey to Becoming an Author

The highs, lows, and everything in between on my path to publication.

By I.E. Jones III

🌄 "The Dream That Wouldn't Let Go"

Some people discover writing by accident. I discovered it the way you discover breathing — slowly, then all at once.
Stories lived in my head long before I had the courage to put them on paper. I scribbled ideas in notebooks, on napkins, in the margins of school assignments. I didn't know it then, but those scattered fragments were the first stepping stones on a path I'd spend years learning how to walk.

Becoming an author wasn't a single decision. It was a series of small, stubborn choices to keep going.

✍️ "The First Attempts — And the First Rejections"

My early writing was… enthusiastic. Not polished, not structured, but full of heart. I wrote entire chapters with no plot, characters who changed names halfway through, and endings that arrived simply because I ran out of steam.

When I finally gathered the courage to submit my work, the rejections came fast and unceremoniously.
Some were form letters.
Some were kind.
Some stung more than I wanted to admit.

But each one taught me something:

  • My voice needed sharpening
  • My craft needed discipline
  • My stories needed truth

Rejection didn't break me. It shaped me.

📚 "Learning the Craft — The Humbling Middle"

There's a moment every writer faces when passion alone stops being enough.
For me, that moment arrived when I realized I didn't just want to write — I wanted to write well.

So I studied:

  • Books on structure
  • Essays on character
  • Interviews with authors I admired
  • My own drafts, with brutal honesty

I learned to revise without mercy.
I learned that inspiration is a spark, but craft is the fire.

And slowly, my writing began to change.
Not overnight.
But undeniably.

🌧️ "The Lows — Doubt, Burnout, and the Quiet Moments of Quitting"

Every journey has valleys, and mine had plenty.

There were days I questioned everything:

  • Was I good enough?
  • Was this worth it?
  • Was I chasing a dream that didn't want me back?

I quit writing more times than I can count.
But the stories never quit me.

They whispered.
They tugged.
They waited.

And eventually, I always returned to the page.

🌤️ "The Highs — Breakthroughs, Community, and Small Victories"

The highs didn't arrive in dramatic fashion.
They came quietly, like dawn.

A critique partner who believed in my work.
A chapter that finally clicked.
A character who felt alive.
A beta reader who said, "I couldn't put it down."

These moments were small, but they were fuel.
They reminded me why I started.
They reminded me who I was becoming.

🏆 "The Moment Everything Changed"

Publication didn't happen with fireworks.
It happened with an email.

A simple message that said:
"We'd like to move forward."

I read it three times before it felt real.

After years of doubt, drafts, rewrites, and resilience, someone saw value in the story I had fought to tell. It wasn't just validation — it was a quiet, powerful affirmation that persistence matters.

🚀 "Where I Stand Now"

Becoming an author didn't solve everything.
It didn't erase the fear or silence the doubt.
But it gave me something stronger: momentum.

I write now with a deeper sense of purpose.
Not because I've "made it," but because I understand the journey — the messy, beautiful, unpredictable journey — is the point.

❤️ "What I Learned Along the Way"

If my path has taught me anything, it's this:

  • Write even when it's hard.
  • Learn even when it's humbling.
  • Rest when you need to, but don't quit.
  • Your voice matters — even before anyone hears it.

Becoming an author isn't a destination.
It's a lifelong conversation between you and the stories that choose you.

And I'm grateful every day that I kept listening.
